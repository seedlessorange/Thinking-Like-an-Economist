How suppliers' ability to respond changes with time

Momentary Period

(Market Period: Seconds to Hours)

Characteristics:
  • PES = 0 (Perfectly Inelastic)
  • Supply is fixed
  • No time to adjust production
  • Price absorbs all demand changes
üì¶ Example:

Fresh fish at a market in the morning - the catch is fixed. Increased demand raises price; supply can't respond.

Short Run

(Weeks to Months)

Characteristics:
  • 0 < PES < 1 (Inelastic)
  • Some ability to adjust
  • Variable inputs can change
  • Fixed inputs remain constant
üè≠ Example:

Factory hiring more workers and adding shifts, but can't expand building or buy new machinery yet.

Long Run

(Years)

Characteristics:
  • PES > 1 (Elastic)
  • Full flexibility to adjust
  • All inputs can vary
  • Entry/exit possible
üèóÔ∏è Example:

New factories built, technology upgraded, firms enter/exit market. Full adjustment to demand changes.

Time = Flexibility

The longer the time horizon, the more elastic supply becomes as firms can adjust more factors of production.

üí∞
Price Volatility

When supply is inelastic (short run), prices fluctuate more in response to demand changes.

Policy Implications

Short-run policies may cause large price swings; long-run policies allow smoother adjustments.

üìê Elasticity Formula

PES = % Change in Quantity Supplied / % Change in Price

Higher PES = More responsive supply = Flatter curve
