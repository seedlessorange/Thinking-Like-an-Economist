## Key Concepts Illustrated

### iPhone Case Study Teaches:
1. **Price Skimming:** Start high to capture consumer surplus from inelastic segment
2. **Third-Degree Price Discrimination:** Separate markets by time
3. **Demand Elasticity Changes:** Early adopters (inelastic) → Mainstream (elastic)
4. **Consumer Backlash Risk:** Moving too fast can damage brand trust
5. **Crisis Management:** $100 credit as goodwill purchase
6. **Strategic Learning:** Apple's shift to stable pricing + product tiering

### Cereal Case Study Teaches:
1. **Penetration Pricing:** Start low to overcome trial barriers
2. **Experience Goods:** Quality unknown until consumed
3. **Habit Formation:** Switching costs increase over time
4. **Demand Elasticity Evolution:** Elastic (launch) → Inelastic (maturity)
5. **Market Share Strategy:** Low price drives volume for shelf space
6. **Brand Building:** Loyalty allows gradual price increases

### Comparison Highlights:
- **Opposite strategies for different market conditions**
- **Both exploit changing price elasticity**
- **Product uniqueness determines which strategy to use**
- **Competitive environment matters**
- **Brand strength affects pricing power**

## Economic Principles Demonstrated

1. **Consumer Surplus:** The gap between WTP and actual price
2. **Price Discrimination:** Charging different prices to different segments
3. **Elasticity of Demand:** How quantity responds to price changes
4. **Dynamic Pricing:** Adjusting prices over product lifecycle
5. **Network Effects:** Value increases with user base (iPhone)
6. **Switching Costs:** Habit formation creates stickiness (cereal)
7. **Market Segmentation:** Early adopters vs. mainstream
8. **Brand Equity:** Trust and loyalty as economic assets
