## Additional Insights from "Who Thinks Like an Economist" Document

I also reviewed the Mentimeter responses document. Key roles that think like economists in the private sector:

### Strategic Roles:
- **CEOs and C-suite executives** - Allocate scarce resources, maximize value under constraints
- **Management consultants** - Apply cost-benefit analysis, trade-off thinking
- **Business strategists** - Assess market dynamics, competition, incentives
- **Investment bankers and financial analysts** - Optimize capital allocation, risk-return trade-offs

### Operational Roles:
- **Product managers** - Balance revenue vs. market share, elasticity considerations
- **Pricing teams** - Dynamic pricing, price discrimination, elasticity analysis
- **Supply chain managers** - Optimize resource allocation, minimize costs

### Analytical Roles:
- **Data scientists** - Economic modeling, demand forecasting
- **Market researchers** - Consumer behavior, willingness to pay
- **Quantitative analysts** - Financial modeling, asset pricing

This reinforces that economic thinking is about:
1. **Scarcity** - Limited resources, tough choices
2. **Trade-offs** - Every decision has opportunity costs
3. **Incentives** - Understanding what drives behavior
4. **Marginal analysis** - Comparing incremental benefits vs. costs
5. **Market equilibrium** - Supply-demand balance
