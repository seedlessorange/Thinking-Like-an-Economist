Understanding Market Power

When firms become dominant in their markets, they gain the power to influence prices rather than simply accepting market prices. This fundamentally changes how they make decisions.

1. Gains from Trade
Total Surplus Formula

Total Surplus = Consumer Surplus + Producer Surplus

The total economic value created by market transactions. This represents the "pie" that gets divided between consumers and producers.

The Amazon Case

Amazon's argument: It expands the total surplus through:

1 Lower Prices

Efficiency and scale drive prices down, increasing consumer surplus

2 Vast Selection

Millions of "long-tail" items satisfy niche demand that physical stores couldn't serve

3 Convenience

One-click ordering and fast shipping save time, which has real economic value

Result: Even if Amazon captures a large share, it dramatically increases total gains from trade.

2. What Makes a Firm "Dominant"?
Dominant Firm Definition

A firm with a large market share that can influence prices. The critical question: "What is the relevant market?"

Is Amazon Dominant? It Depends on Market Definition

YES - E-Commerce View

  • 40-50% of US e-commerce market
  • 30-40% of global cloud computing (AWS)
  • Acts as "gatekeeper" for sellers
  • Forces competitors onto its platform

NO - Total Retail View

  • Only ~10-15% of total US retail
  • Competes with Walmart, Costco, Target
  • Just one large competitor
  • Not a monopoly in broader context
Key Insight

Market definition determines whether a firm is "dominant." This is why antitrust cases spend so much time debating market boundaries!

3. Monopoly Profit Maximization

The Fundamental Difference

Unlike competitive firms, monopolies don't take the market price. They have market power to set prices.

How Monopolies Choose Price & Quantity

1 Find optimal quantity

Produce where Marginal Revenue (MR) = Marginal Cost (MC)

This is where profit is maximized

2 Find the price consumers will pay

Look at the Demand curve at that quantity

This gives the highest price consumers will accept

3 Result

Price is always higher than MC (unlike perfect competition where P = MC)

Monopoly Markup

4. The Marginal Revenue Formula

For Linear Demand Curve

If Demand: $$P = a - bQ$$

Then: $$MR = a - 2bQ$$

Same intercept, twice the slope

Why?

When you sell one more unit, you gain revenue from that unit BUT you have to lower the price on all previous units. This "revenue loss on infra-marginal units" makes MR steeper than demand.

Deadweight Loss (DWL)

Monopolies create deadweight loss - trades that would benefit both buyers and sellers but don't happen because the monopoly restricts output to raise prices. This represents lost social welfare.

Graphically: The triangle between the monopoly quantity (Qm) and competitive quantity (Qc) represents trades that society loses out on.

Session 7 Key Takeaway

Dominant firms have market power that allows them to charge prices above marginal cost, earning higher profits but creating deadweight loss. The key question in antitrust cases is often: "What is the relevant market?" Market definition determines whether a firm is truly dominant and whether its behavior harms social welfare.
