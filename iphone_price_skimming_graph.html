The Price Discrimination Dilemma

Apple's iPhone pricing illustrates the fundamental tension in price discrimination: You want to capture consumer surplus from different segments, but doing it too obviously or too quickly can damage brand equity and customer loyalty. The $100 store credit was effectively Apple buying back some goodwill - reducing their profit from early adopters but preserving the long-term value of customer trust.

Moore's Law paradox: In tech, prices must fall due to exponential cost reductions. But fall too fast, and you anger your best customers. Fall too slow, and competitors undercut you.
