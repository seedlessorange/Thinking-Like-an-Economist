üîÑ Two-Sided Market Characteristics

1
Cross-Side Network Effects

More users make the platform more valuable to advertisers. More advertisers fund better free services for users.

2
Asymmetric Pricing

Users pay $0 (subsidized side). Advertisers pay billions (money side). Google profits from the difference.

3
Platform as Intermediary

Google doesn't produce content or products - it connects the two sides and facilitates transactions.

4
Winner-Take-Most Dynamics

Network effects create high barriers to entry. Google dominates with 92% search market share (2024).

Why This Model Dominates the Digital Economy

Google's two-sided market model creates a virtuous cycle: Free services attract users ‚Üí More users attract advertisers ‚Üí Ad revenue funds better services ‚Üí Even more users join ‚Üí Stronger network effects ‚Üí Nearly impossible for competitors to break in.
