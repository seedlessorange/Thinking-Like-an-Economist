Why This Matters for Competition

ğŸ­ High MES Industries

Examples: Automobiles, aircraft, semiconductors

  • Must produce massive volume to reach MES

  • Natural tendency toward oligopoly
  • Few large firms dominate

Result: Markets with 3-10 large competitors (e.g., Boeing, Airbus)

ğŸ• Low MES Industries

Examples: Restaurants, retail stores, services

  • Can achieve minimum AC at small scale
  • Low barriers to entry
  • Many firms can compete
  • Fragmented markets

Result: Highly competitive markets with many small firms

Key Takeaway

The shape of the LRAC curve determines market structure:

  • Industries with high MES naturally become concentrated (few large firms) because small firms cannot compete on cost
  • Industries with low MES remain fragmented (many small firms) because size provides little cost advantage
  • New entrants must reach MES quickly or face a fatal cost disadvantage
  • This is why Tesla had to scale rapidly - below MES, they couldn't compete with Toyota, VW, etc.
