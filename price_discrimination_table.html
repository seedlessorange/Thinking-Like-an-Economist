üéØ Strategic Choice: Which Degree to Use?

1st Degree

Use when: One-on-one negotiations possible, high-value transactions, each customer unique

2nd Degree

Use when: Can create product tiers, volume matters, customers self-select naturally

3rd Degree

Use when: Clear observable segments, different elasticities, arbitrage preventable

üí° Practical Reality: Most firms use a combination of 2nd and 3rd degree discrimination. First-degree is the theoretical ideal but rarely achievable in practice. The key is to identify which customers have higher willingness to pay and extract more surplus from them without losing lower-value customers entirely.

‚ö†Ô∏è Three Necessary Conditions for ALL Price Discrimination

1. Market Power

Firm must be price maker, not price taker. Perfect competition eliminates this possibility.

2. Prevent Arbitrage

Low-price buyers can't resell to high-price buyers. Services work best (can't resell haircut!).

3. Different Elasticities

Customer segments must have different price sensitivity. Otherwise, no point in discriminating.

Critical Note: If even ONE of these conditions fails, price discrimination cannot work. All three are necessary.
