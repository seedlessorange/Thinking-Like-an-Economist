üéØ Why Penetration Pricing Works for Cereal

1
Commoditized Market

Cereal is not revolutionary - it's entering a crowded market with many substitutes. Unlike iPhone, there's no novelty premium to capture.

2
Experience Good

Quality can only be assessed after trying. Low price removes the risk barrier and incentivizes trial. Once consumers taste it and like it, switching costs increase.

3
Habit Formation

Breakfast cereal is a habitual purchase. Once consumers develop a routine (e.g., "I eat Brand X every morning"), they become less price-sensitive. The habit is the switching cost.

4
Market Share Battle

In grocery, shelf space matters. Higher sales volume ‚Üí better shelf placement ‚Üí more visibility ‚Üí more sales. Low initial price drives volume that justifies premium shelf space.

5
Brand Equity Building

As brand recognition grows and loyalty develops, the demand curve becomes steeper (more inelastic). This allows gradual price increases without losing customers.

Dynamic Pricing = Exploiting Changes in Elasticity

Both strategies work because they adapt prices to changing demand elasticity:

  • iPhone: Started with inelastic (enthusiasts) ‚Üí dropped price when demand became elastic (mainstream)
  • Cereal: Started with elastic (unknown) ‚Üí raised price as demand became inelastic (loyalty)

The optimal strategy depends on whether you're moving down-market (iPhone) or building up-market (cereal). Choose based on your product's uniqueness and competitive position.
